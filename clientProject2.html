Expert Pass

IMPROVEMENT PROJECT

Expert Pass is a is an annual subscription platform with content about the financial world. It has the purpose of letting people earn their independence when it comes to investing, providing exclusive courses, reports, lives with analysts, recommendations of actives, and exclusive customer service.

Problem

After 1 year after the launch, the churn rate was up to 45%, with an average of 27.5% per month. Intending to downgrade the churn to 20%, we seek to understand the possible improvements.

Challenges

Investigate the reasons for users' cancellation and what are improvement points throughout the user's journey we can do.

My role

Creating the script for the user research, analysing the data. Responsible for applying the results to a strategy and executate UI and UX improvements.

Key Insights

By looking through the NPS score on the platform we could already have a glimpse of the issues. We conducted user research focusing on these points to investigate the reasons for cancellation and to understand the pain points that the users could be going through.

Both users that canceled and users that are still subscribed presented the same main pain points. These insights helped me determine how to approach solutions.

Some topics appeared strongly in the research, regarding content and customer service.

“I can find the same content for free”

“Content is superficial”

“The content does not match my expectations”

“I contacted customer service and the problem was not solved”

“When I have a question about the content and I send an email, the response time is not satisfactory”

Regarding the platform navigation and experience, the main issues were:

“Platform is confusing, I can't find what I need to”

“It's difficult to undersantd everything that there is on the site”

”The way that the content is shown falls short”

Strategy

As shown in the research, the main issue was related to the content. I had to think about in which ways we could create a roadmap and create a backlog to work alongside the stakeholders responsible for producing the content.

Even though the main issue was related to content, there was still room for improvements in the experience as the research showed. So with that in mind, I started to analyze the flows and mapped the main pain points in design to make improvements.

Other major pain points that were passed along to other departments:

  • Improve the customer service (chat) and the problem resolutions by capacitating the attendants.
  • Analyze all data already collected in the SAQ to review the FAQ.
  • Investigate cases where subscriptions were canceled automatically and where the credit card of some banks was not accepted.
  • Better defines exclusivity of the contents, not leaving exclusive contents in the open area (not paid) of the website.
  • Deepen studies to understand the content problem shown in the research by doing an in-depth interviews with users.

Solutions in design

01.
Create tools to facilitate the navigation and findability of contents.

02.
Improve responsivity in the devices, providing mechanisms to turn the experience more fluid.

03.
Run UX tests every 3 months to keep understanding users' needs.

Improving the navigation

As users shown some difficulties when navigating, we made some UI improvements such as carousel visualization and scroll, and improvements in the menu and profile.

I also made sure that there was a better responsivity for the website, regarding tech development.

Finding content was never so easy

To facilitate the findability of the content - a big pain point shown in the survey - a personalization of content was created based on the client's level of interest. Thus generating a fluid, organic navigation, and an efficient filtering mechanism.

Login

One of the problems identified internally was the login that was not indexed in google - the customer had difficulties finding the login page.

Building a new profile area

I could also track some problems related to settings in the profile area, with that, we redesigned a new version of that area, enabling proper edition and configuration of the information displayed.
